Andrea Adams, Ph.D., J.D., M.B.A.
Summer Institute of Computational Science Fellow
Andrea Adams is an Associate Professor and Program Coordinator of Crime, Justice, and Security Studies at the University of the District of Columbia (UDC). A licensed attorney specializing in employment and labor law, she leads a multidisciplinary research agenda that explores the ethical intersections of data privacy, emergency management, and gender-based violence. 
Dr. Adams attended the SICSS Summer Institute in 2022 and acted as a fellow for the institute in 2023. She has established a distinguished record of scholarship, including leading externally funded projects for District of Columbia government agencies such as the Mayor’s Office of Returning Citizen Affairs and the Department of Health Care Finance. Her international collaborations focus on using digital technologies to address sexual violence in rural contexts, and she currently serves as the Co-Chair of the Higher Education Ethics Special Interest Group.

As a specialist in pedagogical innovation, Dr. Adams is a certified online instructor dedicated to integrating computational social science (CSS) into criminal justice nd homeland security curriculum. She leads the development of the UDC AI Lab and spearheads its integration with the CSS framework, which, when opened, will utilize R-based methodologies to train students in the use of ethical AI, focused on bias mitigation and transparency in research. Integrating computational social science with AI technologies will help students to use systems thinking and reproducible protocols to address novel security and social problems. Through her leadership in UDC’s Faculty Senate and her role as Co-CEO of the Red Dot Foundation Global, Dr. Adams continues to bridge the gap between ethical theory and technical application in the digital age.
The following works highlight the application of computational methods and data-driven ethics to social justice and security issues:
●	Digital Technologies and Interventions Against Gender-Based Violence in Rural India Areas (2021): Explores the role of smartphone platforms and crowdmapping in addressing sexual violence.
●	Beyond Social Media Push Strategies: Incorporating Citizen-developed Social Media Pull Tactics to Supplement Disaster Communication and Response (2024): Analyzes the transition from passive to active data-gathering models in emergency management.
●	Assessing Women’s Transit Safety in Two Rural Contexts: The Cases of India and Sweden (2025): A cross-cutting chapter utilizing survey data and comparative analysis to identify safety vulnerabilities in transportation infrastructure.
●	Teaching the Code of Ethics to Advance Emergency Management Higher Education Curricula (2025): Focuses on the "implementation fidelity" of ethical standards through measurable pedagogical data.
