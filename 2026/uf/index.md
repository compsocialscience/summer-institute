From June 15 to June 19, 2026, the Summer Institute in Computational Social Science (SICSS-UF) will be held in person at the University of Florida. The Summer Institute aims to bring together graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and beginning faculty interested in computational social science. The Summer Institute is for both social scientists and data scientists, broadly conceived. The SICSS-UF focuses on large language models, social media data, text analysis, networks, and ethics.

The instructional program will involve lectures, group problem sets, and participant-led research projects. In addition, invited speakers will share their experiences conducting computational social science research in a variety of settings. Topics covered include LLM-based text classification and survey item generation, multimodal LLM analysis, model validation, image analysis, machine learning, and research ethics. Participants will have ample opportunities to discuss their ideas and research with organizers, fellow participants, and visiting speakers. Consistent with our commitment to open and reproducible research, all materials created by faculty and students for the Summer Institute will be released as open source.

Participation is open to advanced undergraduate students, graduate students, post-doctoral researchers, and early-career faculty. We welcome applicants from all backgrounds and fields of study, especially those from groups currently underrepresented in computational social science. About 20 participants will be invited, and participants are expected to attend and fully participate in the one-week program.



Application materials are due on May 1.
